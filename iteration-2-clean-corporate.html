Cybersecurity Questions, Answered

Everything you need to know about getting started with professional cybersecurity protection for your small business.

Cybersecurity costs for small businesses vary based on size and needs. Our services start with a free, no-obligation assessment to identify your specific risks. Most SMBs invest between $500–$3,000/month for comprehensive managed security — a fraction of the average cost of a single data breach ($4.45 million in 2023). We offer tiered packages so you only pay for what you actually need, and we're transparent about pricing from day one.

Initial setup typically takes 2–4 weeks depending on your current infrastructure. Our process begins with a free assessment (1–2 days), followed by a custom security roadmap. Core protections — including firewall hardening, MFA setup, and endpoint security — can be active within the first week. Comprehensive monitoring and employee training programs are fully deployed within 30 days.

We specialize in small to mid-size businesses (SMBs) with 5 to 500 employees. Whether you're a startup, professional services firm, retail business, healthcare practice, or growing enterprise, we tailor our cybersecurity solutions to your specific industry, risk profile, and budget. No business is too small to protect — in fact, smaller businesses are increasingly targeted precisely because they often lack strong defenses.

Our free cybersecurity assessment includes a surface-level vulnerability scan of your external-facing assets, a review of your current security posture, identification of your top 3–5 risk areas, and a brief written summary report with prioritized recommendations. The consultation takes about 30 minutes of your time. There is no obligation, no sales pressure, and no hidden fees — just honest information about where you stand.

Yes. We support compliance with HIPAA (healthcare), PCI-DSS (payment card data), SOC 2 (SaaS and service companies), NIST Cybersecurity Framework, and CMMC (government contractors). Our compliance services include gap analysis against the relevant standard, security policy development and documentation, audit preparation support, and ongoing monitoring to maintain your compliance posture. We help you avoid costly fines and protect your business's reputation.

Antivirus is just one layer of a multi-layered security strategy — and it's often the thinnest layer. Modern cyberattacks, including ransomware, phishing, credential theft, business email compromise (BEC), and insider threats, regularly bypass traditional antivirus. A comprehensive cybersecurity program includes network monitoring, employee security awareness training, multi-factor authentication, incident response planning, regular vulnerability assessments, and more. Think of antivirus as a lock on your front door — helpful, but far from sufficient on its own.
